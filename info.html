How to Use This Website

Learn about the crash data and how to read the charts

Interactive Map of NYC Crashes

This map shows real crash locations in New York City. Each red dot is where a crash happened. You can zoom in and click on dots to see more details.

Map Controls: Use your mouse to zoom in or out and drag to move around the map. Click on red dots to see crash details.

Crash Details Cards

View detailed information about individual crashes. Each card shows crash details and includes a button to zoom to that crash location on the map above.

Click "Load Flash Cards" to see information about the crashes.

Step 1: Understanding the Data

The website shows data from New York City. Each crash report includes where the crash happened, when it happened, what caused the crash, and how many people were hurt.

Step 2: Reading the Charts

Bar Chart: Shows crash numbers as bars. Taller bars mean more crashes.

Pie Chart: Shows crashes as pieces of a pie. Bigger pieces mean more crashes.

Both charts show the same information in different ways for the user to understand.

Step 3: Using the Analysis Page

First, go to the Analysis page

Wait for the data to load from NYC

Choose "Bar Chart" or "Pie Chart"

Click "Show Chart" to see the data

Look at the statistics below the chart

What You Will Learn

Which borough has the most car crashes

Which borough has the fewest crashes

How crashes are spread across the city

Tips for Reading Data

Compare the numbers: Look at which borough has the highest and lowest numbers.

Think about why: Areas with more people usually have more crashes.

Look for patterns: Do certain boroughs always have similar crash numbers?
